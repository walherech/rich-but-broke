The Reality

You operate with
clinical precision.
Your finances don't.

Medicine trains you to master the human body. It trains you nothing about the tax code, entity structures, or capital allocation. That gap is costing you a fortune — quietly, every single year.

The advisors you trust are generalists. The strategies you're using were designed for W-2 employees, not high-earning professionals. And the IRS isn't going to send you a reminder.

  • 01 The tax bracket trap. Earning more moves you into rates designed to keep wealth from compounding. Without restructuring, every raise costs you more than you keep.
  • 02 The late start penalty. Med school, residency, fellowship — you began investing a decade after your peers. That gap compounds into millions by retirement.
  • 03 The liability exposure. Malpractice insurance covers your practice. Nothing covers your personal wealth from predatory litigation — unless you structure it right.
  • 04 The advisor problem. Most financial advisors are compensated to sell, not to optimize. The strategies that serve them rarely serve you.
